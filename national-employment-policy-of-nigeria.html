Employment Policy

National Employment Policy of Nigeria: Objectives, Measures, Strategies and Institutional Framework

A Strategic Framework for Promoting Job-Rich Growth, Decent Work, and Inclusive Employment Opportunities in Nigeria

1. Executive Summary

The National Employment Policy (NEP) of Nigeria provides a comprehensive framework for addressing unemployment, underemployment, and labour market inefficiencies in the country. The policy aims to promote productive and decent work by creating an enabling environment for employment-intensive economic growth across key sectors of the economy. It recognizes that while Nigeria has experienced significant economic expansion, this growth has not translated into sufficient job creation or inclusive development.

The policy therefore adopts a multi-sectoral approach to employment generation by aligning macroeconomic policies, sectoral development strategies, and labour market reforms. It emphasizes private sector–led job creation, human capital development, skills acquisition, and improved labour market institutions as central pillars for sustainable employment growth. The policy also highlights the importance of strengthening education–industry linkages, promoting entrepreneurship, expanding infrastructure, and improving access to finance for businesses.

This report is designed primarily for:

❖Federal, state, and local government policymakers responsible for employment and economic development

❖Ministries, Departments, and Agencies (MDAs) involved in labour, education, enterprise development, and economic planning

❖Private sector organizations and business leaders seeking to expand employment opportunities

❖Employers’ associations, professional bodies, and labour unions

❖International development partners and multilateral institutions working on employment and labour market reforms

❖Researchers, policy analysts, and academics studying labour markets and economic development

❖Civil society organizations and non-governmental organizations engaged in employment promotion and social protection

2. Methodology & Analytical Framework

The National Employment Policy is informed by extensive diagnostic studies, labour market assessments, and consultations with key stakeholders across Nigeria. Two major studies—an employment mapping and institutional coordination assessment, and a review of constraints to inclusive and job-rich growth—provided the empirical basis for the policy review.

The analytical framework combines demand-side and supply-side labour market analysis. The demand side focuses on sectors with potential for job creation such as agriculture, manufacturing, infrastructure development, and services, while the supply side examines the role of education, training systems, skills acquisition, and workforce demographics. The policy also incorporates international labour standards and the concept of decent work promoted by the International Labour Organization (ILO).

3. Key Insights & Major Findings

1.Nigeria’s economic growth has been largely jobless, with GDP expansion not translating into sufficient employment opportunities.

2.A large proportion of employment occurs in the informal sector, where income levels and job security are generally low.

3.There is a significant mismatch between the skills produced by the education system and the skills demanded by the labour market.

4.Youth unemployment and underemployment remain major challenges, given Nigeria’s rapidly growing working-age population.

5.Poor infrastructure and limited access to finance constrain private sector growth and employment creation.

6.Weak coordination among government programmes has led to duplication of employment initiatives and inefficient resource utilization.

7.Strengthening human capital development, entrepreneurship, and sectoral value chains is critical for sustainable job creation.
